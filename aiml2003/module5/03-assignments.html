Deliverable 1: Module 5 Lab Lab

Overview

In the readings and in class, you learned that LLMs hallucinate because of how they generate text, and that bias in language models reflects (and sometimes amplifies) patterns in training data. This lab puts those ideas to work. You will build an evaluation harness that tests Gemini on factual questions with known answers, scores its responses using the LLM-as-judge technique, probes for gender associations in profession-related text, and measures sentiment differences across demographic groups.

The bias scorecard in Cell 7 is where the evaluation becomes actionable. Individual tests produce numbers; the scorecard combines them into a visual summary that shows at a glance where the model performs well and where it doesn't. This is the artifact you'll present on Tuesday.

What to Submit

  • A completed Colab notebook (.ipynb) saved to your GitHub repo at week6/lab5_evaluation.ipynb.
  • All cells executed with visible outputs (don't clear outputs).
  • Each code cell preceded by a markdown cell with your LLM prompt and fix note.
  • No API keys or secrets anywhere in the notebook.
  • Submit the GitHub link in Canvas. If it's not in the repo, it doesn't exist for grading.
Start this in Thursday lab. We'll work through the evaluation design together: writing good factual questions, setting up the LLM-as-judge scoring prompt, and running the first profession-gender probe. Come with your 20 factual questions drafted (Cell 2) — we'll review them as a group and fix the ones that are too easy or too ambiguous.

Deliverable 2: Module 5 Presentation Presentation

Overview

A three- to five-minute presentation during Tuesday's class (April 28, 5:30 PM). You may use your notebook as your visual aid, a few slides, or a combination. The goal is to communicate your findings, not to walk through code line by line.

What to Cover

  1. Evaluation design (30 seconds): What categories did you test? Why those categories? What makes a good factual evaluation question?
  2. Factual accuracy results: Show the per-category accuracy breakdown. Which categories did Gemini handle well? Where did it hallucinate? Show one or two specific examples of hallucinated answers and classify the error type.
  3. Bias findings: Show the profession-gender bar chart and the sentiment gap analysis. Did you find bias? How large was it? What surprised you?
  4. The scorecard: Show your combined bias scorecard (Cell 7). Walk the audience through what it reveals at a glance.
  5. Limitations: What didn't your evaluation test? If you had more time, what would you add?

Format

Share your screen. You can present from your notebook, slides, or both. If you attend via Zoom, have your materials open and ready before your turn.
