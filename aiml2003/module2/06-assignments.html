Deliverable 1: Module 2 Lab Lab

Overview

In the readings and in class, you learned that TF-IDF converts raw text into sparse numerical vectors by weighing word frequency against document frequency. This lab puts that idea into practice. You'll build a TF-IDF + logistic regression sentiment classifier on the NLTK movie reviews corpus, then compare it head-to-head against Gemini zero-shot and context-engineered classification on the same 50 test examples.

The comparison is the point. TF-IDF counts words; Gemini encodes meaning in a completely different way. Both can reach similar accuracy on this task, but they fail on different kinds of reviews, and those differences tell you something about what each approach actually understands about language.

Deliverable 2: Module 2 Presentation Presentation

Overview

A three- to five-minute presentation during Tuesday's class (April 7, 5:30 PM) about what you learned building the sentiment classifier. This is not a formal slide deck; think of it as a short show-and-tell for your classmates.

What to Cover

In three to five minutes, walk us through:

  1. The comparison: Show one specific example where TF-IDF and Gemini disagreed on a review. What does each approach "see" that the other misses?
  2. The coefficients: Show your coefficient bar chart. Pick one word with a high positive or negative coefficient and explain what it means about the training data.
  3. The failure: Show a review where one or both classifiers were confidently wrong. Why did it fail?
  4. Your takeaway: Based on your results and the readings, what does this experiment tell you about the difference between counting words and understanding language?

Format

You can share your screen and walk through your notebook, use a few slides, or just talk: whatever communicates your points clearly in three to five minutes. If you attend via Zoom, be prepared to share your screen.

Tip: Practice timing yourself. Five minutes is shorter than you think. Focus on one or two good insights rather than trying to cover everything.
