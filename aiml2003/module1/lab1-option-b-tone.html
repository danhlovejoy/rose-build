Lab 1, Option B: Tone Detector for Social Media

Build a tone classifier that distinguishes between sarcasm, sincerity, and other intentions beyond sentiment analysis.

The Task

Sentiment analysis (positive/negative/neutral) is a solved problem for most models. Tone is harder. A sarcastic tweet and a sincere tweet can both be "negative" in sentiment, but they're doing different things. You'll build a tone classifier that distinguishes between sarcastic, sincere, promotional, rage-bait, and informational posts, then see how structured context changes the model's accuracy on ambiguous cases.

Your Dataset

Collect or write 12-15 social media posts. You can pull real examples from X/Twitter, Reddit, or product review sites, or write your own. Include at least 2 of each tone:

Tone categories

  • Sarcastic: "Oh great, another update that breaks everything. Thanks so much." The words sound positive. The meaning isn't.
  • Sincere: "This actually helped me understand transformers. Bookmarked." Genuine, no hidden layer of meaning.
  • Promotional: "Just dropped our new AI course. Link in bio. Use code LEARN25 for 20% off." Selling something, even when it doesn't look like it.
  • Rage-bait: "Unpopular opinion: college is a scam and everyone who disagrees is coping." Designed to provoke engagement, not to inform.
  • Informational: "The transformer architecture was introduced in the 2017 paper 'Attention Is All You Need.'" Neutral delivery of facts.

The hard cases are posts that could plausibly be two tones. Include at least 3-4 of these. A post like "I can't believe this AI wrote my entire essay in 10 seconds" could be sincere amazement, sarcasm, or even promotional depending on context.

# Cell 3: Approach 1 - Zero-shot
def detect_zero_shot(text):
    response = client.models.generate_content(
        model="gemini-2.0-flash",
        contents=f"Classify the tone of this social media post as one of: "
                 f"sarcastic, sincere, promotional, rage-bait, informational. "
                 f"Respond with only the label.\n\nPost: {text}"
    )
    return response.text.strip().lower()

zero_shot = [detect_zero_shot(p["text"]) for p in posts]
# Cell 4: Approach 2 - Few-shot
def detect_few_shot(text):
    prompt = """Classify the tone of each post as: sarcastic, sincere, promotional, rage-bait, or informational.

Post: "Wow, love how the app crashes every time I need it most." → sarcastic
Post: "Genuinely impressed by how fast this model runs." → sincere
Post: "Check out our new toolkit. Free trial at example.com." → promotional

Post: "{text}" →"""
    response = client.models.generate_content(
        model="gemini-2.0-flash",
        contents=prompt.format(text=text)
    )
    return response.text.strip().lower()

few_shot = [detect_few_shot(p["text"]) for p in posts]
# Cell 5: Approach 3 - Context-engineered prompt
import json

TONE_PROMPT = """## System Instruction
You are a social media analyst specializing in tone detection.
You distinguish between what words literally say and what the
author actually means. You're especially good at catching
sarcasm, disguised promotion, and engagement bait.

## Task
Classify the tone of the social media post below. Consider:
the literal meaning, likely author intent, rhetorical devices
(irony, exaggeration, provocation), and whether the post is
trying to sell something.

## Categories
- sarcastic: says one thing, means the opposite or uses irony
- sincere: genuine expression without hidden intent
- promotional: selling a product, service, or personal brand,
  including subtle self-promotion
- rage-bait: deliberately provocative to generate engagement
- informational: neutral delivery of facts or explanations

## Output Format
Respond with ONLY valid JSON:
{{"tone": "sarcastic" | "sincere" | "promotional" | "rage-bait" | "informational",
  "confidence": "high" | "medium" | "low",
  "signals": "list the specific words/patterns that led to your classification",
  "alternative": "if confidence is medium or low, what other tone could this be?"}}

## Calibration Examples
Post: "Sure, let me just mass-produce a PhD thesis with ChatGPT. That'll go well."
{{"tone": "sarcastic", "confidence": "high",
  "signals": "'Sure' opener, hypothetical scenario presented as absurd, 'That'll go well' is verbal irony",
  "alternative": "none"}}

Post: "We just launched v2.0 and honestly I'm really proud of what the team built."
{{"tone": "promotional", "confidence": "medium",
  "signals": "Announcement of a product launch, pride framing is common in founder posts",
  "alternative": "sincere - if the author isn't affiliated with the product"}}

Post: "Hot take: if you still write code without AI you're basically doing calligraphy"
{{"tone": "rage-bait", "confidence": "high",
  "signals": "'Hot take' framing, absolutist claim ('basically doing calligraphy'), designed to provoke disagreement",
  "alternative": "none"}}

## Constraints
- If the post could genuinely be two tones, pick the more likely
  one but set confidence to "low" and name the alternative.
- Posts under 5 words are hard to classify. Use confidence "low".

## Post to Classify
{post}"""

def detect_context(text):
    response = client.models.generate_content(
        model="gemini-2.0-flash",
        contents=TONE_PROMPT.format(post=text)
    )
    try:
        result = json.loads(response.text)
        return result.get("tone", "error").lower()
    except json.JSONDecodeError:
        return "parse_error"

context_results = [detect_context(p["text"]) for p in posts]
# Cell 6: Approach 4 - LLM-assisted refinement
errors = []
for p, pred in zip(posts, context_results):
    if pred != p["tone"]:
        errors.append(
            f'Post: "{p["text"]}"\n'
            f'  Predicted: {pred}\n'
            f'  Your label: {p["tone"]}'
        )

error_report = "\n\n".join(errors) if errors else "All predictions matched."

meta_prompt = f"""You optimize prompts for tone classification.
Below is a tone detection prompt and the posts it misclassified.

## Current Prompt
{TONE_PROMPT}

## Misclassified Posts
{error_report}

## Your Task
1. Diagnose why each error occurred.
2. Propose a revised prompt that fixes these without
   breaking posts that already work.
3. Return ONLY the complete revised prompt."""

refinement = client.models.generate_content(
    model="gemini-2.0-flash",
    contents=meta_prompt
)
print("=== SUGGESTED REFINEMENTS ===")
print(refinement.text)
# Cell 7: Comparison table
print(f"{'Post':<60} {'Label':<14} {'Zero':<14} {'Few':<14} {'Context':<14}")
print("-" * 116)
for p, zs, fs, ce in zip(posts, zero_shot, few_shot, context_results):

    print(f"{short:<60} {p['tone']:<14} {zs:<14} {fs:<14} {ce:<14}")

for name, results in [("Zero-shot", zero_shot), ("Few-shot", few_shot),
                       ("Context", context_results)]:
    correct = sum(1 for p, r in zip(posts, results) if p["tone"] == r)
    print(f"{name}: {correct}/{len(posts)} ({100*correct/len(posts):.0f}%)")

What Makes This Option Interesting

Tone is subjective in ways that sentiment isn't. Your ground-truth labels reflect your interpretation. You and a classmate could label the same post differently and both be right. This means the lab becomes partly about how well you can teach the model your definition of each tone category through the context-engineered prompt.

The "signals" field in the structured output is especially useful. It forces the model to show its reasoning, and you can check whether it noticed the same rhetorical cues you did. When it misclassifies a post, the signals tell you why: maybe it treated a genuine product announcement as rage-bait because of strong language, or missed sarcasm because the literal meaning was positive.

For your reflection: Focus on the ambiguous posts, the ones where confidence was "low." Did the context-engineered prompt handle ambiguity better than the baselines? What happened when you asked the model to improve its own prompt? Did it change the category definitions, the examples, or the decision rules?
