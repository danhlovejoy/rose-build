Ethics Thread: Where Does the Data Come From?

AIML 2003: Week 1 Discussion. Post by Tuesday, March 31 (before class)

The Prompt

The Karpathy video mentions that LLMs are trained on massive amounts of text scraped from the internet. The 3Blue1Brown video shows how that data shapes the model's internal representations.

This raises a fundamental question about where the training data comes from and who decided what was in it.

In your post, address at least one of the following:

  • When a model is trained on "the internet," whose voices are represented and whose are missing? What are the consequences of that?
  • If an LLM generates text based on patterns in its training data, is the output "original"? Who owns it?
  • Should the people who created the training data (writers, artists, developers) have had a say in how it was used? Why or why not?
  • You're about to use an LLM for class assignments. What's one ethical question you think you should be asking yourself as you do?
Requirements
  • Your post: 150–250 words. Take a position. Don't just summarize the question.
  • Reply to a classmate: At least one substantive reply (not just "I agree"). Engage with their reasoning: push back, build on it, or offer a different angle.
  • Deadline: Initial post by Tuesday, March 31 (before class). Reply by the following Tuesday class.

This is the first of a recurring ethics thread that runs alongside the technical content all semester. Thoughtful answers differ from lazy ones. I'm looking for evidence that you've considered the question, not that you've landed on the "correct" position.
