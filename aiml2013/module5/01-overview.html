What's in This Module

  1. Readings & Prep: The Gender Shades study (Buolamwini & Gebru), a StatQuest refresher on precision/recall/F1, and a reading on intersectional bias in computer vision. Together, these explain why per-group evaluation matters, how to compute the right metrics, and what real-world audits look like.
  2. Module 5 Lab: CV Metrics & Demographic Bias Audit. Fine-tune a MobileNetV2 classifier on CelebA gender labels, compute overall and per-group metrics, run an intersectional analysis, build per-group confusion matrices, create a bias audit scorecard, and display a failure gallery of misclassified images.
  3. Combined Presentation: The Human Cost of Data. For dual-enrolled students. A single presentation covering both the CV demographic accuracy audit and the NLP bias evaluation.
  4. Assignment Sheet: Deliverable details, abbreviated rubrics with Canvas links, and the due date for Tuesday, April 28.
  5. Ethics Discussion: This week's ethics prompt on what happens when accuracy gaps map to real people.
Tip: This module is more about what the metrics mean than how to compute them. The code for accuracy, precision, and recall is a single sklearn function call. The hard part is choosing the right slicing attributes, interpreting the intersectional results, and deciding what the numbers mean for deployment. Spend time understanding the Gender Shades study before Thursday lab — it is the template for what you are building.
