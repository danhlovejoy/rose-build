The Task

In the readings and in class, you learned that edge detection kernels find boundaries by measuring how quickly pixel values change. This week you will build on that: instead of applying a single kernel to visualize one image, you will extract a compact feature descriptor from every image in a dataset and use those descriptors to retrieve visually similar images.

The descriptor is called HOG (Histogram of Oriented Gradients). It works by dividing an image into small cells, computing gradient direction and magnitude in each cell, and summarizing those orientations as a histogram. The result is a fixed-length vector that encodes the local edge structure of the image in a way that is partially robust to small changes in lighting or position.

You will build HOG descriptors, use them for similarity retrieval, and systematically find where the approach fails. Those failures are the lab. They tell you what HOG can and cannot represent, and that is exactly what you need to know before next week.
