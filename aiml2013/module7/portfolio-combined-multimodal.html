Combined Portfolio: Multimodal Portfolio

One presentation. Two courses. One synthesis project that connects them.

Dual-enrollment portfolio: AIML 2003 + AIML 2013

This combined portfolio is for students enrolled in both AIML 2003 (NLP) and AIML 2013 (CV). You give one presentation covering both courses in 5–7 minutes. It replaces both individual portfolio presentations. If you are enrolled in only one course, use that course's standalone portfolio format instead.

The Assignment

You've spent eight weeks building NLP and CV systems in parallel. Some weeks the courses converged (embeddings, vector databases, bias auditing); other weeks they diverged (agents vs. captioning). Your combined portfolio presentation tells the story of both tracks and, critically, includes one synthesis project that connects them.

What to Cover (5–7 Minutes)

  1. The dual arc (1 minute): Open with how the two tracks paralleled and diverged. Where were the connections strongest? Where did they fully separate? The combined lab weeks (Modules 3–5) are natural touchpoints.
  2. 2–3 highlights (2–3 minutes): Choose your best work across both courses. You don't need to cover every module. Pick projects that show range: one from NLP, one from CV, and one combined (or one that demonstrates a cross-domain insight). For each: what you built, what you learned, what you'd change.
  3. The synthesis project (1–2 minutes): This is what distinguishes the combined portfolio from two separate ones. Present one project or demo that combines NLP and CV. Options:
    • Extend the Universal Search Engine (Module 4): Your combined lab built a ChromaDB instance with both text and image embeddings. Extend it: add a natural language query that retrieves both relevant text passages and related images. Show the results side by side.
    • Multimodal bias audit: Combine your Module 5 work. Show the NLP bias scorecard and the CV bias scorecard side by side. Compare how bias manifests in text generation vs. image classification. This doesn't require new code — it's a comparative analysis using existing outputs.
    • Captioning + RAG: Feed your Module 6 image captions into your Module 4 RAG pipeline. Ask questions about images using text retrieval over generated captions. This is a quick prototype that demonstrates how NLP and CV pipelines can chain together.
    • Your own idea: Any project that meaningfully combines NLP and CV tools or insights from the semester. Clear it with me during Thursday lab if you're unsure.
  4. Reflection (1 minute): What is the most important thing you learned from studying NLP and CV together that you wouldn't have learned from either alone? The mathematical commonalities (vectors, similarity, embeddings, attention) are a good starting point, but the best answers go beyond the math to the conceptual parallels.

Synthesis Project Expectations

The synthesis project does not need to be a polished production system. It can be:

  • A short extension of an existing combined lab (5–10 new cells in a notebook).
  • A comparative analysis using outputs from both courses (no new code, just new analysis).
  • A proof-of-concept that chains an NLP pipeline to a CV pipeline.

What matters is that the connection between NLP and CV is genuine, not forced. A side-by-side comparison of two unrelated notebooks is not a synthesis project. A system that uses text embeddings and image embeddings in the same pipeline is.

Format and Submission

Present during the CV class session (Tuesday May 12, 7:00 PM). The combined presentation counts toward both courses. You may use slides, notebooks, or a combination.

If your synthesis project involves a new notebook, save it to your GitHub repo at portfolio/synthesis_project.ipynb. Submit the same repo link to both Canvas courses.

Combined Reflection

Dual-enrolled students write a single "Synthesis Reflection" (600–1000 words) submitted to both courses. Due Thursday, May 14 by 11:59 PM. In addition to the standard reflection prompts, address: What are the mathematical commonalities between NLP and CV? Where did the two fields diverge in ways that surprised you? What would a unified "AI practitioner" curriculum look like if it combined both?

Tip: The combined portfolio presentation is the capstone of your dual-enrollment experience. The students who do well are the ones who find a genuine insight at the intersection of NLP and CV — not just "both use vectors" but something deeper about how different modalities encode meaning, where the same evaluation techniques apply, and where they break down. That insight is the synthesis.
