Module 6: Generative Vision

AIML 2013 • Introduction to Computer Vision • Spring 2026

Until now, your CV models have classified or retrieved images. This week you flip the direction: instead of turning images into labels, you turn images into language. You'll feed images to a vision-language model, generate captions, and find out what the model invents.

What's in This Module

  1. Readings & Prep: A video on how vision-language models work, the CHAIR hallucination metric paper, and a reading on VLM applications in accessibility and medical imaging. Together, these explain the architecture, the evaluation techniques, and the real-world impact of captioning accuracy.
  2. Module 6 Lab: Image Captioning with Vision-Language Models. Generate captions with Gemini Vision, evaluate object accuracy against reference descriptions, compute per-category hallucination rates, build a hallucination gallery, and test prompt sensitivity.
  3. Assignment Sheet: Deliverable details, abbreviated rubrics with Canvas links, and the due date for Tuesday, May 5.
  4. Ethics Discussion: This week's ethics prompt on VLM accuracy when the audience depends on the description.
