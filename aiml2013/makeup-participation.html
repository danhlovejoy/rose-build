Missed Class: Participation Makeup Assignment

AIML 2013: Introduction to Computer Vision

Due within 48 hours of the missed class session
When does this apply? This assignment is available any time you miss a Tuesday class or Thursday lab session. It replaces the attendance and engagement portion (80%) of your weekly participation grade. You do not need prior approval to use it — just submit it within the deadline.
This does not replace the weekly ethics discussion post. The ethics post is the other 20% of your weekly participation grade and is required whether you attend class or not. If you miss class, you need both this makeup post and the ethics post to earn full participation credit for the week.

Why This Exists

Participation (20% of your grade) is designed to reward engagement with the material and your classmates. Each week it breaks down into two parts: attendance and engagement (80%) and the weekly ethics discussion post (20%).

When you attend class, earning the attendance portion is straightforward: ask questions, respond to classmates, engage in discussions. When you can't attend, this assignment gives you a path to earn those same points.

It's intentionally more work than just showing up, because attending class is the easier and better way to learn. But life happens, and this ensures a missed class doesn't automatically mean lost points.

What to Submit

Post a single, substantive discussion post (minimum 500 words) in the Missed Class Makeup discussion thread in Canvas. Your post must include all three of the following sections:

1. Session Summary

Watch the Zoom recording of the class you missed (available in Canvas under Zoom Recordings) or, if no recording is available, thoroughly review all materials covered that session (slides, readings, videos).

Write a summary of the key concepts, questions, and discussion points from the session. This should demonstrate that you engaged with the full session, not just skimmed it. If you're making up a lab session, summarize what was worked on and any issues or breakthroughs discussed.

2. Classmate Response

If the missed session included student presentations, choose at least one classmate's presentation and respond to it directly: What did they build? What did you find interesting about their approach? What question would you have asked them?

If the missed session did not include presentations (e.g., a Thursday lab), respond to a specific question or discussion point that came up during the session. Explain your perspective and what you would have contributed.

3. Personal Reflection

Reflect on the week's material in your own words. Address:

  • What clicked for you this week? What concept or technique makes sense now that didn't before?
  • What's still unclear or confusing? Be specific.
  • How does this week's topic connect to what we've covered in previous weeks?
Generic or surface-level posts will not receive full credit. I can tell the difference between someone who watched the recording and someone who skimmed the slides for five minutes. The point of this assignment is to engage with the material at the same depth you would have in class.

Logistics

Where to Post

Canvas → Discussions → Missed Class Participation Makeup

Create a new thread for each makeup post. Title it with your name and the session you missed (e.g., "Jane Smith — Week 3 Tuesday").

Deadline

48 hours after the missed class session. For example, if you miss Tuesday's 7:00 PM class, your post is due by Thursday at 7:00 PM. Late posts will not be accepted — the point is to stay current with the class.

How Many Times Can I Use This?

There is no hard limit, but this is a makeup option, not a replacement for attendance. If you find yourself using this regularly, please reach out to me so we can discuss what's going on.

The easy way to earn full participation each week: come to class and post your ethics response. This makeup assignment exists as a safety net, not as the default path. Your classmates and I benefit from having you in the room.
