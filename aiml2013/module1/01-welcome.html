How This Course Works

Each two-week module follows the same rhythm:

Week A: Learn the Concept

  • Before Tuesday: Watch the assigned videos and do the readings (~90 min).
  • Tuesday class In Person: We discuss the concepts. I'll ask questions, you'll ask questions, and we'll make sure everyone understands the "why" before we code.
  • Thursday lab Zoom: We work through the assignment together. This is your best opportunity to get help and debug in real time.

Week B: Apply & Present

  • Before Tuesday: Finish your lab, push it to GitHub, and prepare your presentation.
  • Tuesday class In Person: You present what you built (3–5 minutes). New topic readings/videos assigned.
  • Thursday lab Zoom: We start the next lab together.
  • By Tuesday class Due: Deliverables due in GitHub (link submitted in Canvas) — see the Syllabus for exact dates.
Key point: I grade everything through your GitHub repo, including notebook contents (GitHub renders .ipynb files with all outputs). Save your final work to GitHub before the deadline. If it's not in your repo, it doesn't exist for grading.

How Participation Works (20% of Your Grade)

Each week, your participation grade comes from two things:

  • Attendance & engagement (80%): Show up to class, ask questions, engage in discussion. If you attend via Zoom, keep your camera on and participate in the chat. This is the easy path — just be present and engaged.
  • Weekly ethics discussion post (20%): Each week includes a short discussion prompt about the ethical implications of what we're learning. Post your response and reply to a classmate by the Tuesday class deadline. Details are in each week's module.

If you miss a class session, you can earn the attendance/engagement portion by submitting a Missed Class Participation Makeup post within 48 hours (see that assignment for details). The ethics post is still required separately. Both together = full participation credit for the week.

The easiest way to earn participation points is to come to class and do the weekly ethics post. The makeup path is intentionally more work. Show up, engage, post your ethics response.

A Word About GenAI in This Course

I expect you to use GenAI tools in this class. Computer vision applications are increasingly powered by AI models, after all. But remember: you are accountable for the work you submit. A computer cannot be held accountable. You'll get credit for work you do with AI, and you'll be counted off for errors GenAI tools make.

Two hard rules:

Never share your university login credentials with any external GenAI agent. Do not upload sensitive data, PII, or your school login credentials into any AI tool. Assume all inputs are public and will be used to train future models.
