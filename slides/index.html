AIML Interactive Demos

AIML 2003 (NLP) • AIML 2013 (Computer Vision) — Rose State College, Spring 2026

Inside the Pipeline: TF-IDF + Logistic Regression

Trace a movie review through every step — tokenization, TF-IDF vectorization, the dot product, and sigmoid classification. Type your own text to see how the model processes it.

 Logistic Regression: The Binary Classifier

Drag a decision boundary, watch gradient descent train in real time, inspect individual predictions, and see where the model fails. A two-feature visualization of how binary classification works.

 Cosine Similarity: Measuring How Alike Two Things Are

Drag 2D vectors to build geometric intuition, compare documents by their words, compare images by their edges, and explore similarity matrices. The same math serves both NLP and CV.

 What Do Embeddings Encode?

Every word becomes a list of numbers, and those numbers encode meaning. Explore attribute dimensions, nearest neighbors, and analogy math (king − man + woman) to see how geometry captures semantics.

 Pixel Histograms: How Machines See Light

Load an image, inspect individual pixel values, and watch brightness histograms update in real time. See how grayscale conversion, thresholding, and equalization change what the machine sees.

 HOG: Histogram of Oriented Gradients

Watch an image become a vector. Grayscale, Sobel gradients, cell histograms, the classic HOG visualization, and cosine similarity comparison — all computed live in the browser. Upload your own images.

 Confusion Matrix: Measuring What a Classifier Gets Wrong

A classifier's accuracy is one number. The confusion matrix is the whole story. Adjust thresholds, watch precision and recall move, and see why the same model looks strong or weak depending on which error you care about.

 Neural Networks: Why Layers Matter

A single neuron draws a straight line. Stack neurons into a hidden layer and the network untangles data no line could separate. Train live, watch the decision boundary bend, and inspect what each hidden unit learns.

 Attention: How Every Word Reaches Across the Sentence

Click a word to see which others it attends to. Step through the Q/K/V pipeline, compare attention heads, and watch how swapping one adjective rewires the whole sentence. The mechanism behind transformers.

 Hallucination: Why Models Lie Fluently

A language model doesn't know facts — it predicts the next token. Walk through the stages where confident-sounding errors are born, and see why fluency and truth are not the same thing.
